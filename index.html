About (Me & The app)

This is a full-stack, ML application written over Christmas break to show off my technical skills to recruiters.

I am a software engineer studying computer science at the University of Alberta, due to graduate in May 2025. I primarily consider myself a backend engineer (if it is not obvious through the simplicity of this web page).

This application works by using a neural network to first detect the faces present in an image, and then extract their mathematical representations (this representation is called an embedding). When you have access to many of these embeddings, there is a natural notion of 'distance' between them.

The next key component in the app is a vector database. A database offers a convenient way to store and access some sort of structured data. A vector database is the same, except they are purpose built around aproximate nearest neighbour algorithms. These algorithms help us take advantage of the aforementioned notion of 'distance' when searching a set of embeddings, finding one that is (hopefully) similar to yours.

This process and technology is similar to what a service like Netflix or Spotify could use in their recommendation systems. For example, if you like a certain song, Spotify would query its vector database of songs, and give you suggestions of songs which embeddings are located nearby. This is of course an over simplification, and their production recommendation systems are much more complicated than this.

The Tech Stack

  • Go and gRPC are used for building scalable and efficient APIs that handle communication between services.

  • DeepFace and Python are leveraged for facial recognition and processing, enabling accurate face embeddings.

  • AWS provides the scalable infrastructure necessary to host and manage our application efficiently.

  • Kubernetes orchestrates our containerized applications, ensuring high availability and scalability.

  • Milvus serves as our vector database, enabling efficient storage and retrieval of high-dimensional face embeddings.

  • TailWind CSS provides utility-first classes that streamline our styling process, ensuring a consistent and responsive design.
